Expectations

Job Outlook:

Software Engineering is a rapidly growing career field. The average growth rate for this specialty is about 20% from 2018 --> 2028. So the likelyhood of you being left without job is extremely rare for the foreseeable future (2019).

Education Requirements:

Most software engineering positions require a bachelors degree in computer science or a related field at minimum. However you can also have relavant experience for about the same amount of time it takes for the required degree, and could still land a job. It truly boils down to your proven skill set and how well you can market yourself to your perspective employer. For information on a relavant certification to help you on your journey, click here!

Average Pay:

Pay for this career is largely based on experience and location. For instance, if you are going for an entry level position then you could expect to make about $70k in the midwest compared to $105k in Silicon Valley. The more experience you have, the greater your pay will be as well as additional benefits. These could include company paid helthcare, continued education, or even profit sharing. As an example, you may make a base of $105k with Google in CA, but you could additionally get $50k worth of stocks each year. This would bring your total annual income to $155k.

Work Environment:

Most software engineers work in an office setting with an open floor plan. Open floor plans at most companies allow for greater communication and collaboration. If you choose to contract and work for yourself, then your work environment can be anything you wish it to be. You are only limited by your imagination.
